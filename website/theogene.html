EDUCATION: A1 WILDLIFE MANAGEMENT

Since he was young, he feared alterations in weather patterns that make him always thinking beyond about his contributions to the community for adapting and mitigating the change in weather by integrating wildlife management, community welfare, agriculture and sustainable development in Rwanda. Subsequently, he did his secondary studies in veterinary technology, he has awarded by Rwanda education board to keep studying in Kitabi College of Conservation and Environmental Management in faculty of wildlife Management. Therefore, next to completing his studies, he did academic Internship in Karisoke Research Center where his research topic was the assessment of bamboo shoots regeneration and its Impacts to the golden monkey’s distribution in Volcano National Park. Not only that but also he was working with the department of biodiversity research where they used to collect data on aquatic Insect, Birds, Plants diversity as he kept on learning. At the end of the internship, he has received an applicable medal as Intern who performed well his duties than the others, and he has got a job for four months to assist researcher who was assessing plant diversity in burnt by comparing unburnt area in Muhabura volcano found in Volcano National Park. After that Biodiversity Research Manager in Kalisoke Research centre gave me the opportunity of being a professional intern for Three months where he was in charge of collecting data about water fetching in Volcano National Park and its impact to both local community and biodiversity in volcano National Park. Afterlife in Kalisoke Research Center, he went back to his community with initiative of helping his community to adapt and mitigate climate change where he did community education on conservation agriculture in his sector as he was ambitious to modal farmer so that he can get the point of extensions his young generation to develop their selves building on what they have with adapting and mitigating climate change. Moreover, he did a Biodiversity Survey in Rugezi wetland with International Crane Conservation, Rwanda development Board, Kitabi College of Conservation and Environmental Management. Furthermore, he did training on Project planning and management by National Geography Additionally, he got an opportunity by Ange Imanishimwe, Co-Founder of BioCoop Rwanda, to be a volunteer for only one month. Since January 2019 to July 2019, he was Research Assistant in Bio Coop Rwanda, Currently he is Biodiversity Conservation and Ecotourism manager at BioCoop Rwanda where they tackle on Conservation in Action by Integrating Biodiversity Conservation, Leadership, Entrepreneurship, Climate Protection, and Community Development through Natural Ecosystems restoration and rehabilitation in Nyungwe National Park, Rwanda. All in all, his dreaming is about amalgamation for creating green Job as well as innovative research on adaptation and mitigation measures to change in weather patterns focusing on Sustainable development of local communities.
