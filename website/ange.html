BIOCOOP RWANDA TEAM

Dr. Ange Imanishimwe

Email:angeish07@gmail.com
Phone contact:+250788840755
Education: PhD Student in Biodiversity Conservation

Ange Imanishimwe, from his young age, he committed to creating a good change in his community by integrating biodiversity conservation, community health, agriculture, entrepreneurship, climate protection, and sustainable development in Rwanda. Named Top Young Innovator of Rwanda, 2012, he founded and serve as President of BIOCOOP. he has worked in The Nature Conservancy (www.tnc.org) as a short term Consultant and he is the founding member and Vice President of Kuzamura Ubuzima, an NGO working in health, nutrition, and agriculture operating in Butare Referral Hospital (kazumuraubuzima). In this role, he negotiated nearly 100,000 USD in grants and investments from the South Korean government, UNDP, and others to support my community. he has served as a Tourist Guide in Nyungwe National Park at the Rwanda Development Board for four years (RDB). he has earned BSc in Zoology and Conservation and he has MSc in Biodiversity Conservation. He is a PhD Student in Biodiversity Conservation in the University of Rwanda. His goal is to eradicate extreme poverty and malnutrition in Rwanda by creating 1,000 green jobs every year. He has created the social businesses to promote the sustainable development of Youth in Rwanda. In BIOCOOP they have the vision of putting Rwanda to the level of middle income countries by integrating biodiversity conservation, ecotourism, climate protection, community health, natural resources management, and sustainable development of Rwanda. He completed the training on Civic Leadership and Public Policy in the University of California, Berkeley in Goldman School of Public Policy. When he was in Washington, DC, he asked the question to President Barack Obama on climate change (youtube). he was selected to be among 100 African Young Leaders to do the Professional Development Experience in the USA and his placement was in The Nature Conservancy in Boston, Massachusetts (youtube). He contributed to the community conservation program in Yale University, School of Forestry and Environmental Sciences (vimeo). The Mandela Washington Fellowship connected him to the professionals in Biodiversity Conservation and Environmental Management. He was invited by Yale University in Connecticut and Antioch University New England in New Hampshire to give the presentations of my achievements in Rwanda. He was highly trained on Climate Change Mitigation and adaptation in Kenya, Germany, and Sweden. He is now the Consultant of Biodiversity study and monitoring in Nyungwe National Park buffer zone.
