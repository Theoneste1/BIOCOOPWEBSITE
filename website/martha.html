Mrtha Dusabamahoro

COMMUNITY GUIDE AND RECEPTIONIST AT BIOCOOP Rwanda
Email:mahororitha@gmail.com
Phone contact:+250786517666
Skype:mahoromaritha

Dusabyamahoro has a passion to protect wildlife fauna, help to understand its role for life and learn to use conservation for sustainable development. She is committed to bringing about positive change in the world, starting with her community. She grew up in the countryside, where most people, including her family, worked in subsistence agriculture but could hardly put a meal on the table, and where children dropped out of school at an early age to earn money or for early marriages. Seeing this, She studied hard intending to establish a steady income and a wide range of practices towards sustainable economic solutions for a rural community to make a living while staying in harmony with nature and its benefits. In secondary school, she selected the scientific track of Biology, Chemistry and Geography, and in university, She studied Wildlife and Aquatic Resource Management, where she graduated top of the class in 2017. Shortly after, she met her current employer, Ange Imanishimwe, at a conservation forum. She was inspired by his presentation where he stressed that "conservation requires collective responsibility." and realized that she wanted to play a part in wildlife and habitat conservation. She now works for BIOCOOP Rwanda Biodiversity Conservation Company & Kitabi Ecocenter as a receptionist and nature tour guide. Her conservation focus is on pollinators (bees, butterflies and birds) as they play a critical role in making life possible on this planet. Her passion is to connect economic opportunity with habitat conservation within her community.
